# Token-only

Use this file for raises where the investor is buying exposure to a token or future token rather than equity in the operating company.

## When token-only is appropriate

Choose `Token-only` only when:
- the token is genuinely part of the product, network, or protocol design
- the project can defend utility, distribution, incentives, and governance logic
- the team is ready for token-specific diligence
- the audience is suited to token-risk and token-market structure

This is not the default pre-market route. It demands stronger discipline around utility, compliance posture, operations, and communications.

## Goal and audience

The deck must sell participation in a network economy, not ownership in a conventional company. The audience is usually crypto-native funds, strategic ecosystem participants, or specialized investors.

## Recommended size

Use roughly `14-20 slides`, plus separate supporting materials such as:
- token memo or white paper
- technical appendix
- legal or compliance summary
- security and launch-readiness materials

The deck should not try to carry the full legal or technical burden alone.

## Recommended slide order

1. `Title + what the network or protocol is`
2. `Problem in current system`
3. `Solution / architecture`
4. `Why now`
5. `Market and adoption path`
6. `Product status: prototype, testnet, roadmap`
7. `Token utility and mechanism design`
8. `Supply, emissions, distribution, vesting, lockups`
9. `Security and operations`
10. `Regulatory and compliance posture`
11. `Go-to-market / ecosystem bootstrap`
12. `Team`
13. `Roadmap and milestones`
14. `Token sale terms`
15. `Risks and mitigations`

## What must be explicit

### The token is part of the product

Make the utility unavoidable, not ornamental. Explain how the token participates in:
- access
- fees
- staking or security
- governance
- incentives

### Token design

Investors expect clarity on:
- total supply or supply logic
- issuance or emissions model
- allocation philosophy
- insider and community balance
- vesting and unlock schedule
- treasury logic
- value accrual mechanics, described carefully

### Operating readiness

Token raises need operational credibility:
- smart contract readiness
- audits or audit plan
- treasury and custody logic
- launch sequencing
- restrictions, gates, and controls around distribution

### Compliance posture

The deck should show that the team knows there is a compliance perimeter and is not winging it. Depending on the context, that can mean:
- legal classification work
- AML and KYC process
- distribution restrictions
- marketing restrictions
- disclosure documents outside the deck

The deck is not a white paper or legal memo. Treat it as the investor narrative layer.

## Financial model at token stage

A token-only deck still needs a real resource plan. Show:
- network-activity assumptions
- token-economic assumptions
- fiat runway for development and compliance work
- milestone-based use of proceeds

Even token teams spend real currency before network effects arrive.

## Language guardrails

Do not use deck language that implies:
- guaranteed profits
- passive income promises
- certain token appreciation
- inevitable exchange listing
- easy liquidity

Anchor the language in utility, usage, ecosystem design, security, and operating constraints.

## Common mistakes

- using tokenization as a financing gimmick rather than product logic
- overfocusing on hype and underexplaining adoption path
- showing allocation without vesting discipline
- omitting operational and security readiness
- treating community size as the same thing as durable demand
- forgetting fiat runway and execution dependencies
- publishing token-investor language that is unsafe for broad circulation

## DD support to prepare

Useful token-only follow-up materials:
- token memo or white paper
- legal classification summary
- AML and KYC process notes
- security and smart-contract audit plan
- treasury and custody approach
- detailed tokenomics appendix
- roadmap to mainnet or TGE
- risk register and mitigation plan
