# Copywriting Style: Mikhail / Misha Hypov (LiteFinance)

## Author Profile

**Mikhail Hypov** — independent trader and analyst, MBA in economics, trading since 2007 on Forex, MICEX, and crypto markets. Regularly ranks in TradingView top crypto analyst ratings. Has been running free online courses since 2017.

**Role in text**: experienced mentor sharing knowledge with a student. Not a professor reading a lecture — but a senior colleague explaining things at the same desk.

---

## Tone and Voice

**Friendly expert.** Warm but authoritative. The reader feels a knowledgeable person nearby, not faceless corporate text.

- Address as "you" in English, without formality
- Frequently uses "we" — creates sense of joint analysis: "Let's figure out...", "Now let's look at..."
- Honest about limitations and risks: does not hide tool drawbacks
- Balance of optimism and caution: gives hope but warns
- Personal experience as argument: "With years of experience in crypto trading, I have seen..."

**What is absent**: familiarity, aggressive marketing, exaggerations, empty promises.

---

## Article Opening

**Three typical variants:**

1. **Personal greeting + topic announcement:**
   > "Dear friends! Today we will look at one of the most popular indicators..."

2. **Direct question to reader:**
   > "Do you want to determine entry points in the market accurately?"

3. **Thesis + context:**
   > "Cryptocurrencies are becoming increasingly popular among investors."

After the opening — immediate value proposition: what the reader will learn and why they need it.

---

## Mandatory Article Structure

```
H1: [Keyword]: [What it is] [and how to use/trade]

Major Takeaways          ← 4-6 short points, the most important
Table of Contents        ← navigation for long article

What is it?              ← definition + context
How does it work?        ← theory + formulas (if needed)
How to use it?           ← practice, strategies, steps
Real examples            ← specific charts/scenarios
Settings / parameters    ← table (if applicable)
Pros and Cons            ← Pros/Cons table
Conclusion               ← brief summary + motivation
FAQ                      ← 5-10 frequent questions
Related materials        ← links to related topics
```

---

## Logic of Material Presentation

**Principle: from general to specific, from theory to practice.**

1. What it is and where it came from (history/creator)
2. How it works inside (mechanics/formula)
3. How to read signals (interpretation)
4. How to trade (specific strategies with entry/exit points)
5. Limitations and typical mistakes
6. Summary + FAQ

Each section logically follows the previous. The reader never gets lost.

## Typical Phrases and Expressions

**Transitions and introduction:**
- "Let's figure out..."
- "Let me explain..."
- "Let's now look at..."
- "Below we will discuss..."
- "Let us begin with..."
- "In this section, we will cover..."

**Internal references:**
- "As we mentioned above..."
- "As I described earlier..."
- "Let me remind you once again..."
- "Recall that..."

**Emphasis on important:**
- "It is important to note that..."
- "Please remember..."
- "As a rule..."
- "In practical terms..."

**Personal position:**
- "I recommend..."
- "In my experience..."
- "I have compiled a personal rating..."
- "With years of experience, I have seen..."

**Chart demonstrations:**
- "You can see in the chart above..."
- "In the example below..."
- "As shown in the figure..."
- "Look at the highlighted area..."

**Step-by-step instructions:**
- "Open a long position at the close of the candle."
- "Set the stop loss just below the previous local low."
- "Follow these steps:"
- "Place a stop loss. Exit when..."

## Formatting

### Lists
- **Numbered** — for step-by-step instructions (strategies, algorithms)
- **Bulleted** — for listing signals, signs, factors
- Maximum 6-7 points in one list — longer ones split into subsections

### Tables
- Mandatory for comparisons: **Pros / Cons** with two columns
- For indicator settings: parameter → value → description
- For tool comparison: X vs Y by several criteria

### Text Emphasis
- **Bold** — term at first mention, key settings, important numbers
- "Important!" block / ">" quote — critical information, risk warnings
- Acronym explained at first occurrence: "MACD (Moving Average Convergence/Divergence)"

### Visual Elements
- Each strategy illustrated with a chart (real pairs: EURUSD, GBPUSD, XAUUSD, BTCUSD)
- Charts have color annotations (blue = entry point, red = stop-loss, green = take-profit)
- Minimum 10-15 screenshots/charts per full article

### Paragraphs
- Short: 2-4 sentences maximum
- One paragraph = one idea
- Between major sections — horizontal divider `---`

---

## Working with Terminology

- Technical term + explanation in context or parentheses
- Never leaves a term unexplained at first occurrence
- Balance: professional language + accessible explanation
- Specific numbers always better than vague estimates: "$13 → $1,100" instead of "sharp growth"
- References to creators/history add authority: "developed by Gerald Appel in 1979"

---

## Examples and Evidence

- Historical precedents with specific dates and prices (BTC 2013, 2017, 2020-2021)
- Real trading pairs, not abstract examples
- Numeric thresholds: "80–90% of cases", "161.8% Fibonacci level", "364 points profit"
- Multiple markets to demonstrate universality (Forex, crypto, stocks)
- Step-by-step trade breakdowns with exact entry, stop, and target points

---

## Article Ending

**Three elements of the finale:**

1. **Summary** — 2-3 sentences with main conclusions
2. **Motivating call to action** — without aggression, with faith in the reader:
   > "Start using Butterfly patterns and discover new ways to make a profit!"
   > "Just give it a try and see for yourself!"
3. **Risk Warning block** — short disclaimer before signature

**FAQ section** — 5-10 questions, answers what the reader will definitely ask after reading. Questions go from simple to complex.

---

## Major Takeaways Structure

Always 4-6 points at the very beginning. Each point is a complete thought that provides value on its own. The reader can read only this block and leave with benefit.

Example:
```
## Major Takeaways

- The MACD indicator measures momentum by comparing two EMAs.
- A signal line crossover above zero indicates bullish momentum.
- Best settings for day trading: 12, 26, 9.
- MACD works best on trending markets, not flat ones.
- Combine with RSI or volume for stronger signals.
```

## What to Avoid

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| Abstract promises "earn more" | Specific strategy with numbers |
| Complex academic text without explanations | Term + contextual explanation |
| "Some traders believe..." | "In my experience..." / specific source |
| Long paragraphs of 8-10 sentences | Maximum 4 sentences per paragraph |
| Text only without visualization | Each concept → screenshot/diagram |
| Complete certainty in forecasts | Honest "it is not possible to guarantee..." |
| Ignoring risks | Always mention tool limitations |

## Content Types and Their Features

### "What is X" — educational articles
- Start with definition in first paragraph
- History of the tool/concept
- Mechanics of operation
- Practical application
- Comparison with alternatives

### "How to use X" — practical guides
- Start with result ("after reading you will be able to...")
- Step-by-step instructions with numbering
- Real examples for each step
- Typical mistakes + how to avoid them

### "X vs Y" — comparative reviews
- Key differences table at the beginning
- Analysis of each by the same criteria
- Clear conclusion: when to use what

### Market analysis / forecasts
- Historical context (previous cycles)
- Technical and fundamental factors
- Specific levels and scenarios
- Honest caveat about uncertainty
